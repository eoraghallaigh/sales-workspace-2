# Prospecting Management \- Research Report

The book of business initiative requires reps to prospect differently. Instead of developing their own systems, workflows and targets for prospecting into companies, reps are now required to use the sales workspace to methodically work through P1 to P4 companies and meet clearly-defined engagement/outreach criteria. For this to work effectively, sales managers need to be able to monitor their teams’ performance against these criteria. 

Right now, managers can use Looker for this. But we believe that we can provide more value by designing an integrated experience into the sales workspace. In addition, if managers are using the workspace regularly then they will also help drive adoption of it amongst their teams.

To build the most useful tool possible, we need to understand how managers in the BoB pilot currently monitor and manage the prospecting performance of their teams.

## Research Objectives

1. Understand how sales managers define effective prospecting.

2. Understand how sales managers coach their staff to prospect effectively.

3. Understand how sales managers hold staff accountable to performance standards regarding prospecting.

## Key findings

### **1\. Defining Effective Prospecting**

Managers define effective prospecting as composed of three main factors: 

1. **Structured planning**

   1. Effective reps block time for prospecting and know in advance how they are going to use that time. 

   2. They know when they are going to work reactive vs proactive and install base vs net new.

   3. They prepare call lists the night before so they can execute immediately during power hours (call blocks).

2. **Strategic targeting**

   1. Managers emphasized that intent does not always equal fit. Effective reps filter for high-fit accounts (employee size, industry) and prioritise those over low-quality high-intent leads.

   2. Actioning recent conversions and intent signals quickly is important IF those leads are also high fit.

   3. Writing personalised emails and linkedin messages is still valued.

3. **Depth of work**

   1. High performers do not just contact one person; they map the account and reach out to multiple decision-makers (e.g., CMO, VP of Sales, CEO) simultaneously. They aim for 3-4 distinct touches per account rather than "spraying and praying" one touch across many accounts.

   2. Top performers use creative methods like Loom videos to break through, rather than relying solely on automated sequences.

| Insight:  Unlike deal management, outbound prospecting doesn’t have clearly defined steps to walk though. It’s a self-directed activity that can easily go off the rails if the rep isn’t organised. Having a structure means that reps don’t waste time thinking about what to do next. It makes their prospecting less chaotic and stressed. *“I am really, really big on like with my reps, their process and like their operating rhythm... the reps that are very good at prospecting, like, they're very good at time management. I think that's the ultimate thing that it comes back to, which i do think is a really big gap, especially in Small Biz.”* |
| :---- |

| Opportunity:  BoB helps with targeting and depth of work, but not really with structure or time management. What can we do to help reps be more organised in their planning? |

### **2\. Key Metrics & Monitoring Routines**

While managers monitor activity, they primarily focus on outcome-based metrics to judge effectiveness.

* **RPA (Results, Pipeline, Activity):** This is the standard framework used across almost all managers interviewed. This is often a Friday team meeting or Monday 1:1 where reps self-report their numbers or review a dashboard together. They look at Results (Quota attainment) first; if that is missed, they look at Pipeline generation; if that is low, they look at Activity.

* **Deal Creation & Pipeline Gen:** The most trusted indicator is deal creation and the dollar value of pipeline generated. One manager targets 3–3.5x pipeline coverage relative to quota.

* **Activity Volume:** Managers generally expect a baseline of volume, such as 30–40 calls per day or 15 minutes of actual talk time per day.

* **Depth of Work Rate:** Managers want to see how thoroughly an account is worked (e.g., touches per account) rather than just whether it was touched once.

* **Team Visibility:** Managers frequently display dashboards in team settings to foster healthy competition and transparency, celebrating top performers to motivate others.


**"Tripwires" for Intervention:**

* **Lack of Deal Creation:** If activity is high but no deals/meetings are being booked, managers want to intervene immediately.

* **Low Talk Time:** A rep making high dials but having very low talk time suggests they are "gaming" the system or calling bad numbers.

* **Pipeline Consistency:** Managers look for consistency in pipeline generation week-over-week rather than sporadic spikes.

| Insight:  Managers are still reactive rather than proactive. They look for results, and only intervene if a problem becomes apparent via lagging indicators (e.g. rep hasn’t closed enough MRR to keep them on pace for the month).   *“If they have the results and they have the pipeline, we never really talk about activity.”* |
| :---- |

| Opportunity:  How can we help managers be a little more proactive, and spot problems even earlier? We need to make sure managers can easily see when a rep is not doing high quality outreach, which puts their pipeline generation at risk. |
| :---- |

| Insight:  Small Business is a fast moving segment with daily KPIs. Managers will monitor metrics multiple times a day and will check in with reps multiple times a week if necessary to ensure they are meeting their activity expectations. At the same time, we need to make sure the activity is high quality.   *“We're really pushing them that the minimum that we need is 30 phone calls per day. It would be great if there was a trigger, an alert sent to them and the manager saying, uh-oh, we only did 22 dials today. Looks like we're going to need 38 tomorrow” “If somebody's doing 45 calls and they've only got two minutes talk time that's a... big concern.”* |
| :---- |

| Opportunity:  Currently the workspace only shows a rep monthly metrics. Let’s help reps and managers understand the reps’ daily activities and pacing towards their daily and weekly goals, while maintaining quality of outreach.  |
| :---- |

### **3\. Coaching & Diagnostics**

When performance lags, managers shift from monitoring data to qualitative observation.

* **Shadowing / "Ride-Alongs":** Because data cannot always reveal why a rep is failing (e.g., bad messaging vs. bad list), managers sit on Zoom calls to watch reps prospect in real-time. This helps identify if the rep is unprepared or fearful of calling decision-makers.

* **Skill vs. Will:** Managers use data to diagnose the root cause. If activity is high but results are low, it is a skill issue (messaging/targeting). If activity is low, it is a will issue (effort).

* **Peer Coaching:** Managers encourage top performers to share specific tactics (e.g., "Winner of the Week") or host co-working sessions ("Power Hours") where the team prospects together.

| Insight:  Ridealongs and deep dives are helpful but time-consuming. Managers do them because there is no better way to see exactly what a rep is doing. How are they prioritizing companies, what are they writing in their emails, which specific contacts are they reaching out to? There is no other way to find these things out other than ridealongs.  *“No report is going to tell you that. They can fudge numbers at the end of the day and hit all their call targets, the vanity numbers. Ridealongs are the only way around it... I'm gonna sit and watch you and it's gonna be creepy and then we're gonna fix it.”* |
| :---- |

| Opportunity:  Can we surface more of these deep qualitative insights to tell the story behind the RPA numbers? Surface these to both the rep and the manager ahead of time to reduce the need for ridealongs or make them more focused? |
| :---- |

### **4\. Current Tools & Workflows**

Managers are currently cobbling together views from multiple sources, often relying on spreadsheets due to limitations in official reporting tools. They do this not because they all want slightly different reports, but simply because they haven’t been given anything standardised. 

* **Fragmentation:** Managers use a mix of Portal 53 dashboards, Looker reports, and manual spreadsheets/Google Docs to track performance.

* **Looker vs. Portal 53:** Managers generally prefer Portal 53 for real-time data and actionability, noting that Looker can be slow, latent (24-hour delay), or inaccurate.

* **Manual Tracking:** Because goals cannot always be built into Portal 53 reports, managers resort to Excel to track progress against targets.

| Insight:  Managers use all these different tools not because they all think they have their unique magic formula for tracking performance. It’s simply that they haven’t been given a standardised tool, apart from RPA. If they had standardised reports, they would gladly focus on those. *“I would love to have somebody go, we’re building this. This is going to be your single source of truth. Now going forward with BOB, scrap all the other reports and all the other lookers.”* |
| :---- |

| Opportunity:  There is a significant opportunity to consolidate and standardize these reports. |

### **5\. Book of Business (BoB) Specific Findings**

The transition to BoB has introduced new expectations, but visibility into adherence is currently poor.

* **Lack of SLA Visibility:** Managers reported a significant gap in tracking BoB specific SLAs (e.g., contacting P1s within 24 hours). They generally cannot see this data easily today and rely on surface-level activity metrics instead.

* **Distrust of P-Rankings:** Some managers and reps do not fully trust the P1–P4 categorization, feeling that P1s are not always true high-priority leads. Consequently, some reps ignore the P-rankings and rely on their own filtering for intent/industry.

* **Desire for "Depth" Metrics:** Managers explicitly requested views that show "Depth of Work" (how many touches per account) to ensure reps aren't just "dashboard dressing" by logging a single low-quality task to clear a P1.

* **Task Queue Overwhelm:** Reps and managers find the current task-based view overwhelming or low-context, leading some to ignore the "Task" tab entirely in favor of their own lists.
