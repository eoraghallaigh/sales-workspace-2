Our primary users are SMB reps on the new Book of Business (BoB) operating model. Two roles, different contexts.

---

## Growth Specialist (GS)

**Role:** Owns a book of SMB accounts end-to-end — prospects, qualifies, and manages deals to close.
**Daily loop:** mix of reactive (inbound tasks, deal progression) and proactive (outbound to P2 accounts) work.
**Tools:** Sales Workspace, HubSpot CRM, Sales Hub, dialler, email.
**Constraints:**
- Has deals in flight — can't context-switch away from prospecting easily
- Monthly capacity is finite; P2 proactive load competes with pipeline work
- Multi-threading is expected but not well supported

**What they care about:**
- Knowing which accounts are worth time *today*
- Not losing track of accounts after closing a task
- Building momentum across a company, not just hitting one contact

**What frustrates them:**
- Switching between dashboards to reconstruct state
- Bulk-assigned P2 tasks with no clear "where to start"
- Progress that isn't visible to them or their manager

## SDR (Sales Development Rep)

**Role:** Prospects and qualifies accounts, then hands off. Does **not** manage deals.
**Daily loop:** mostly proactive outbound, higher task volume than GS, less deal-state overhead.
**Tools:** same as GS, minus deal management.
**Constraints:**
- Same prospecting automation/process expectations as GS despite not carrying deals
- Higher daily task volume → more sensitive to workflow friction
- Often earlier in career → benefits more from opinionated defaults

**What they care about:**
- Hitting daily activity and outcome targets
- Clear signals on who to reach out to and how
- Confidence that the tasks they're given are worth doing

**What frustrates them:**
- Ambiguous priorities across a big list
- Having to make many small decisions before taking the first action

## Not our users (but often confused for them)

- **AEs (Account Executives) outside BoB** — different book construction, different expectations. Don't over-index on their feedback for BoB features.
- **Mid-market/Enterprise sellers** — workspace needs differ substantially; their input is interesting but not authoritative for SMB design.
